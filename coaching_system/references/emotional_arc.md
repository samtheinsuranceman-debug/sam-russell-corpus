# Emotional Arc Management Reference

## The 7-Phase Prospect Emotional Journey

### Phase 1: Curiosity
- **Indicators:** Questions, leaning in, "tell me more"
- **Sam's job:** Hook them. Be interesting. Keep it SHORT.
- **Danger:** Sam tends to over-deliver here. 2 minutes max.
- **Transition trigger:** Prospect asks a specific question about their situation

### Phase 2: Recognition
- **Indicators:** "That's exactly what happens to us" / nodding / relating
- **Sam's job:** Validate. "You're not alone in this."
- **Danger:** Low. Sam is good here.
- **Transition trigger:** Prospect describes their specific pain

### Phase 3: Pain Awareness
- **Indicators:** Frustration, urgency language, "we've been dealing with this for..."
- **Sam's job:** Acknowledge pain briefly, then MOVE TO SOLUTION.
- **DANGER: THIS IS WHERE SAM GETS STUCK.** He loves explaining the pain so thoroughly that the prospect drowns in it and never reaches Hope.
- **The Rule:** Maximum 3 sentences about the pain. Then pivot: "Here's what we do about that."
- **Transition trigger:** Prospect says "so what can we do?" or any variation

### Phase 4: Hope
- **Indicators:** "Really?" / "That's possible?" / tone shift upward
- **Sam's job:** Paint the picture. Future pace. "In 6 months, here's what it looks like..."
- **Danger:** Sam sometimes kills hope by adding caveats and disclaimers. DON'T.
- **Transition trigger:** Prospect starts asking "how" questions

### Phase 5: Desire
- **Indicators:** Ownership language, timeline questions, pricing questions
- **Sam's job:** LET THEM WANT IT. Don't oversell. Silence is your friend here.
- **Danger:** Sam fills silence with more features. The prospect was already sold.
- **Transition trigger:** Any buying signal (see buying_signals.md)

### Phase 6: Confidence
- **Indicators:** "I trust you" / "You clearly know this" / relaxed tone
- **Sam's job:** ASK FOR THE SALE. They trust you. Close.
- **Danger:** Sam interprets confidence as permission to teach more. IT'S NOT.
- **Transition trigger:** Sam asks for the commitment

### Phase 7: Commitment
- **Indicators:** "Yes" / "Let's do it" / "Send me the paperwork"
- **Sam's job:** STOP TALKING. Process the deal. Confirm next steps in 2 sentences.
- **CRITICAL DANGER:** Sam talks for 23 additional minutes after commitment. This is where deals UNCLOSE themselves.
- **The Rule:** After "yes" → "Great. I'll send [specific thing] by [specific time]. You'll hear from me [when]. Thank you." HANG UP.

## Arc Regression Detection

**What it looks like:** Prospect was at Phase 5 (Desire) but suddenly asks a basic Phase 2 question.

**What caused it:** Sam said something that created doubt. Usually:
- Mentioned a risk or caveat unnecessarily
- Brought up a competitor
- Over-explained a feature that raised new questions
- Used jargon that confused them

**Recovery protocol:**
1. Stop. Breathe.
2. "Good question. The short answer is [2 sentences]."
3. Return to where they were: "So as we were discussing, the next step would be..."
4. Do NOT re-explain everything from the beginning.

## Sam's Emotional Arc Failure Modes (Mapped to Revenue)

| Failure Mode | Phase Where It Happens | Revenue Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Over-explaining pain | Phase 3 → stuck | Prospect overwhelmed, leaves |
| Feature dumping after desire | Phase 5 → regression to 3 | Prospect confused, needs to "think about it" |
| Talking after commitment | Phase 7 → regression to 4 | Deal uncloses itself |
| Killing hope with caveats | Phase 4 → regression to 2 | Prospect loses interest |
| Silence avoidance | Phase 5-6 | Prospect never gets space to commit |

## Emotional State Tracking (Sam's Own Arc)

Monitor Sam's emotional state during coaching:

| State | Indicators | Coaching Response |
|---|---|---|
| Stressed (1-2/5) | Short answers, frustration, "I don't know" | "Pause. What's the real problem? Not the symptom." |
| Neutral (3/5) | Normal engagement, analytical | Standard coaching mode |
| Confident (4/5) | Fast answers, humor, big plans | Challenge him: "That sounds good. What could go wrong?" |
| Excited (5/5) | Rapid speech, multiple ideas, interrupting | Ground him: "Pick ONE. Execute it today. Tell me tonight." |
| Frustrated (variable) | Self-criticism, "I should have..." | Reframe: "What did you learn? How do you use it tomorrow?" |
