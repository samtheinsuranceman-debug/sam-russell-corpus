# Buddy's 200-Question Calibration Study
## Voice-to-Voice Training Session

**Purpose:** These 200 questions are designed to map every dimension of your communication style, values, decision architecture, emotional patterns, representational systems, meta-programs, and behavioral preferences. Once answered, Buddy will model YOUR communication patterns as a primary operating mode — your slang, your rhythm, your values, your way of seeing the world.

**How to Use:** Answer naturally, in your own words. There are no wrong answers. The more authentic and unfiltered your responses, the more precisely Buddy can calibrate. Some questions may seem similar — that's intentional. They approach the same dimension from different angles to build a 3D map of who you are.

---

## SECTION 1: REPRESENTATIONAL SYSTEM MAPPING (Questions 1-25)
*These questions identify whether you process the world primarily through Visual, Auditory, or Kinesthetic channels.*

1. When you think about your most successful business deal, what comes to mind first — do you see the moment, hear the conversation, or feel the rush?

2. When you're explaining a complex financial concept to a client, do you naturally draw pictures, tell stories, or walk them through the feeling of what it's like?

3. If I asked you to describe your ideal future 10 years from now — would you paint me a picture of what it looks like, tell me what people are saying about you, or describe how it feels to be there?

4. When you're making a big decision, do you need to see it laid out visually, talk it through out loud, or go with your gut feeling?

5. Think about a time you were absolutely certain about something. How did you know? Did it look right, sound right, or feel right?

6. When someone's lying to you, what tips you off first — something in their face/eyes, something in their voice/tone, or something that just doesn't feel right?

7. When you remember your childhood home, what comes first — the image of it, the sounds in it, or the feeling of being there?

8. How do you prefer to receive information — charts and visuals, someone explaining it verbally, or hands-on experience?

9. When you're stressed, what bothers you most — things looking chaotic, too much noise/talking, or physical tension and discomfort?

10. Complete this: "I'll believe it when I _____ it." (see/hear/feel)

11. When you meet someone new, what do you notice first — how they look, how they sound, or the energy/vibe they give off?

12. If you're buying a car, what matters most — how it looks, how the engine sounds, or how it feels to drive?

13. When you're angry, does the world look red/dark, do you hear a voice in your head, or do you feel heat/pressure in your body?

14. How do you know when you trust someone — do you see consistency in their actions, hear sincerity in their voice, or feel it in your gut?

15. When learning something new, do you prefer diagrams, lectures/podcasts, or doing it yourself?

16. Describe your perfect vacation — what do you see, hear, and feel there? Which comes first naturally?

17. When you're excited about a new project, do you visualize the outcome, talk about it constantly, or feel energized and restless?

18. How do you remember phone numbers — see them written, hear them spoken, or type them with your fingers?

19. When you're persuading someone, do you show them evidence, tell them a compelling story, or make them feel the urgency?

20. What's your internal experience when you say "I understand" — do you see clarity, hear it click, or feel it land?

21. When you walk into a room of strangers, what do you scan first — faces and body language, conversations and tone, or the overall energy/atmosphere?

22. How do you know when a meeting went well — it looked productive, sounded collaborative, or felt right?

23. When you recall your greatest achievement, is it more like watching a movie, hearing applause, or feeling pride swell?

24. Do you think in pictures, words/sounds, or feelings primarily?

25. When you say "that doesn't add up" — are you seeing numbers that don't match, hearing something that sounds off, or feeling something that doesn't sit right?

## SECTION 2: META-PROGRAMS & DECISION ARCHITECTURE (Questions 26-55)
*These questions map your unconscious filters — how you sort information, make decisions, and motivate yourself.*

26. What motivates you more — moving toward what you want, or moving away from what you don't want?

27. When you set goals, do you focus on what you'll gain, or what you'll avoid losing?

28. Do you prefer having many options and flexibility, or a clear procedure to follow?

29. When evaluating a new opportunity, do you look at the big picture first or dive into the details?

30. Do you need to see proof/evidence before you believe something, or do you trust your own internal sense?

31. How many times do you need to see something work before you're convinced — once, a few times, or consistently over time?

32. Are you more motivated by what others think of you (external validation) or by your own internal standards?

33. When you disagree with someone, do you tell them directly, or find a way to redirect without confrontation?

34. Do you prefer to work independently or collaboratively?

35. When making a decision, do you focus on what's the same as what worked before, or what's different and new?

36. Are you more energized by starting new projects or completing existing ones?

37. Do you tend to think about the past, present, or future most?

38. When you hear a new idea, is your first reaction "yes, and..." or "yes, but..."?

39. Do you prefer to be in charge, or to have someone else handle the logistics while you focus on vision?

40. How do you handle deadlines — do you work steadily toward them, or do you perform best under last-minute pressure?

41. When you explain something, do you start with the conclusion and work backward, or build up to the point?

42. Do you trust people until they prove untrustworthy, or are they untrustworthy until they prove themselves?

43. Are you more motivated by possibility ("imagine if...") or necessity ("you have to...")?

44. Do you prefer variety and change, or consistency and routine?

45. When you receive criticism, do you take it personally or analytically?

46. Do you make decisions quickly based on intuition, or slowly based on analysis?

47. Are you more competitive (beat others) or self-referencing (beat your own standards)?

48. Do you prefer to lead conversations or respond to them?

49. When you're in a group, do you naturally take charge or observe first?

50. How long does it take you to trust someone — minutes, weeks, or months?

51. Do you value loyalty or competence more in the people around you?

52. When you hear "no," does it mean "never" or "not yet"?

53. Are you more motivated by recognition/praise or by money/results?

54. Do you process emotions quickly and move on, or do they linger?

55. When you're wrong, do you admit it immediately or need time to process?

## SECTION 4: COMMUNICATION STYLE & LANGUAGE PATTERNS (Questions 81-115)
*These questions map your natural speech patterns, preferred vocabulary, and conversational rhythm.*

81. When you're fired up about something, what words do you naturally use? Give me 5-10 of your go-to power words.

82. What slang or informal language do you use regularly that most professionals wouldn't?

83. Do you prefer short, punchy sentences or longer, flowing explanations?

84. When you're making a point, do you use metaphors/analogies, data/numbers, or stories?

85. What phrases do you catch yourself repeating? (e.g., "at the end of the day," "here's the thing," "bottom line")

86. How do you naturally open a conversation with someone new?

87. How do you close a conversation — abruptly, with a summary, or with a future hook?

88. When you're teaching someone, do you lecture, ask questions, or demonstrate?

89. Do you prefer to be called by your first name, a nickname, or a title?

90. What tone do you use when you're serious — quiet and intense, loud and direct, or measured and deliberate?

91. How do you express disagreement — head-on, with humor, or by asking questions that expose the flaw?

92. What's your humor style — sarcastic, self-deprecating, observational, or storytelling?

93. When you compliment someone, what do you typically say? Give me an example.

94. When you're disappointed in someone, how do they know — do you tell them, show them, or withdraw?

95. Do you prefer texting, calling, or face-to-face?

96. How fast do you talk normally — rapid-fire, moderate, or deliberate/slow?

97. Do you use hand gestures when you talk? What are your signature moves?

98. When you're listening to someone, what signals do you give that you're engaged?

99. What makes you tune out of a conversation?

100. Do you interrupt people, or wait until they finish? Be honest.

101. When you tell a story, do you start at the beginning, the climax, or the end?

102. What words do you NEVER use? What language feels inauthentic to you?

103. How do you handle silence in a conversation — fill it, or let it breathe?

104. When you're negotiating, what's your opening move?

105. What's your closing move when you want someone to say yes?

106. How do you say "no" to people — directly, with an explanation, or by redirecting?

107. What's your email style — brief bullets, full paragraphs, or voice-note style?

108. When you're excited, does your voice go up, speed up, or get louder?

109. When you're angry, does your voice go quiet, sharp, or loud?

110. What cultural references do you use — sports, movies, music, history, business?

111. Do you curse? If so, what are your go-to words and when do you use them?

112. How do you address a group — formally or like you're talking to friends?

113. What's your signature way of starting a presentation or pitch?

114. When you want someone to feel important, what do you do or say?

115. What's the most persuasive thing anyone ever said to YOU, and why did it work?

## SCORING & CALIBRATION NOTES

After completing all 200 questions, Buddy will map:

| Dimension | What It Reveals |
|-----------|----------------|
| Primary Representational System | Whether to use visual, auditory, or kinesthetic language |
| Meta-Programs Profile | How to frame opportunities (toward/away, big picture/detail, options/procedures) |
| Values Hierarchy | What to emphasize and what to never violate |
| Communication Rhythm | Sentence length, pace, vocabulary level, humor style |
| Emotional Triggers | What to amplify and what to avoid |
| Decision Architecture | How to present choices for maximum buy-in |
| Trust Patterns | How quickly to push, when to back off |
| Identity Anchors | Stories and references that resonate |
| Behavioral Rhythms | When to engage, when to give space |
| Language Fingerprint | Slang, power words, forbidden words, signature phrases |

**This calibration creates a unique "You Model" that Buddy uses as the default communication template for all interactions with you — your words, your rhythm, your values, your way.**
