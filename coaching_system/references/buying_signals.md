# Buying Signal Detection & Amplification Reference

## Signal Categories

### Explicit Signals (35 points each)
- "What's the pricing?"
- "How quickly can we implement?"
- "Can we do a trial?"
- "What are the next steps?"
- "Send me the contract/agreement"
- "How soon can we start?"

### Implicit/Verbal Signals (25 points each)
- Future tense: "When we implement this..."
- Ownership language: "Our team would use..."
- Timeline questions: "How long does setup take?"
- Implementation questions: "What does onboarding look like?"

### Stakeholder Signals (20 points each)
- "I need to run this by my team"
- "My boss would want to know..."
- "Let me check with my partner/spouse"
- "Who else in our industry uses this?"

### Comparison Signals (15 points each)
- "How does this compare to X?"
- "What makes you different from [competitor]?"
- "We're also looking at..."

### Soft Confirmation Signals (10 points each)
- "That makes sense"
- "I can see how that would work"
- "That's exactly what we need"
- Multiple "mm-hmm" or "right" responses

## Intent Scoring Thresholds

| Score | Level | Sam's Action |
|---|---|---|
| 0-30 | COOL | Continue building value, ask discovery questions |
| 31-60 | WARM | Transition toward close, start trial closing |
| 61+ | HOT | CLOSE NOW. Next words = close attempt. Period. |

## Signal Clusters (High Confidence)

### Cluster 1: Evaluation Mode
- Pricing question + competitor mention + timeline ask
- Meaning: Actively comparing, decision imminent
- Sam's move: "It sounds like you're making a decision soon. What would help you decide today?"

### Cluster 2: Internal Selling
- "My team" + "my boss" + feature questions
- Meaning: Already sold, building internal case
- Sam's move: "What does your boss need to see to say yes? I'll put that together for you."

### Cluster 3: Ready to Buy
- Contract terms + implementation + "next steps"
- Meaning: Decision MADE. Working out details.
- Sam's move: "Let's get you started. I'll send the paperwork today."

## Sam's Specific Anti-Patterns

1. **The Feature Dump** — Prospect asks about pricing, Sam responds with 10 minutes of features. FIX: Answer the pricing question in 2 sentences, then ask "Does that work for your budget?"

2. **The Teaching Trap** — Prospect says "that makes sense," Sam interprets as "tell me more." FIX: "That makes sense" = buying signal. Respond with: "Great. Want to move forward?"

3. **The Comparison Spiral** — Prospect mentions competitor, Sam launches into why they're better for 15 minutes. FIX: "The main difference is [one sentence]. What matters most to you in this decision?"

4. **The Stakeholder Misread** — "I need to check with my spouse" is NOT rejection. It's buying. FIX: "Of course. What questions will they have? I can help you present it."

## Amplification Response Templates

### When pricing signal detected:
"Happy to walk through that. To give you the right picture — is this for [scope A] or [scope B]? That affects which option makes sense."

### When timeline signal detected:
"That's doable. For [their date], we'd want to start [specific date]. Want me to map out what that looks like?"

### When stakeholder signal detected:
"Makes total sense. What do you think they'd want to know? I can put together a one-pager, or we could do a quick call together."

### When comparison signal detected:
"Good that you're evaluating options. The main difference clients tell us: [one differentiator]. What's most important to you in this decision?"

### When ready-to-buy cluster detected:
"Sounds like you're ready to move forward. Let me get the paperwork started — what's the best email for that?"
