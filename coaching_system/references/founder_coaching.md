# Founder Coaching Reference

## Sam's Founder Context

Sam is simultaneously building:
1. Russell Capital Solutions (financial advisory, IUL sales)
2. Two AI platforms (patent-protected)
3. A patent portfolio (57+ applications, targeting 100)
4. A sales team (future)

This is NOT a typical single-product startup. This is a multi-front war with one soldier.

## Decision Fatigue Management

Founders make 35,000+ decisions per day. Sam makes more because he's operating across 4 business fronts simultaneously with no team, no driver's license, and probation obligations.

**The 3-Decision Rule:**
- Maximum 3 MAJOR decisions per day
- Everything else gets batched or deferred
- "Major" = irreversible, high-stakes, or resource-committing

**Decision Batching:**
- Group similar decisions into blocks (all patent decisions on Monday, all sales decisions on Tuesday)
- Reduces context-switching cost
- Prevents decision bleed (sales mindset contaminating patent strategy)

**The One-Decision Filter:**
When overwhelmed: "What's the ONE decision that makes the other 10 irrelevant?"
- Example: "Should I hire a VA?" makes irrelevant: scheduling, email management, follow-up timing, calendar conflicts, etc.

## Vision Clarity Protocol

**Monthly Vision Check (5 questions):**
1. "Is what you're building today aligned with where you want to be in 2 years?"
2. "If you could only keep ONE of your four business fronts, which one?"
3. "What would you stop doing if money weren't a concern?"
4. "Who are you building this for — yourself, your dad, your future family, or the market?"
5. "What does 'enough' look like?"

**Mission Drift Detection:**
- Sam drifts when he gets excited about new possibilities (chess tournament pattern — he can WIN at many things, but should he?)
- Flag: "That's interesting. But is it on the critical path to $6M valuation?"
- The chess lesson: Dad told him to redirect his competitive energy toward his career. That was the right call. Apply the same filter to new shiny objects.

## Identity vs. Company Separation

**The Danger:** Sam's identity is fusing with his companies. When the company struggles, Sam struggles. When a deal falls through, Sam feels rejected personally.

**The Reframe:**
- Sam is NOT Russell Capital. Sam is the CREATOR of Russell Capital.
- Sam is NOT his patents. Sam is the INVENTOR who produces patents.
- The company is a vehicle. If it crashes, you build another vehicle. You don't die with it.

**The Test:** "If Russell Capital disappeared tomorrow, who are you?"
- Answer should be: "A closer. A builder. A father's son. A man who creates things."
- NOT: "Nobody. Homeless. A failure."

**Sleeping in the office:** This is identity fusion. The office IS NOT home. The company IS NOT family. This pattern must be monitored.

## Loneliness Management

Founders are isolated. Sam is MORE isolated because:
- No driver's license (can't spontaneously meet people)
- Uber-dependent (every social interaction has a cost barrier)
- Working from office alone (no team, no coworkers)
- Girlfriend relationship is unstable (kicked out multiple times)
- Probation/DUI stigma (social shame barrier)

**Weekly Social Check:**
- "Who did you talk to this week who isn't a prospect, a vendor, or an AI?"
- If the answer is "nobody" or "just my dad" — flag it.
- Isolation accelerates both peak performance AND crash risk.

**The Paradox:** Sam's isolation is FUELING his productivity (no distractions, no social obligations). But it's also building crash risk. The goal isn't to eliminate isolation — it's to add 1-2 genuine human connections per week without disrupting the productivity engine.

## Pivot Decision Framework

**When to pivot (chess thinking):**
- "What's the move that creates the most OPTIONS?" (not the best immediate outcome)
- A move that opens 3 future moves > a move that captures one piece

**Data vs. Gut:**
- When data and gut AGREE: Move fast. High confidence.
- When data says yes, gut says no: Investigate what your gut knows that the data doesn't.
- When gut says yes, data says no: The data is probably right. Your gut is optimistic.
- When both say no: STOP. Don't force it.

**Sam's Specific Pivot Risks:**
1. Abandoning sales for patent work (patents don't pay bills today)
2. Abandoning one AI platform for the other (spreading thin)
3. Chasing a new business idea before current ones are profitable
4. Over-investing in the branding before the revenue justifies it

## Velocity vs. Sustainability

**Sam's Current Velocity:** Unprecedented. Top 0.01% by any measure.

**The Risk:** This velocity is partially fueled by:
- Novelty (new sobriety, new clarity, new AI tools)
- Desperation (homeless, broke, proving himself)
- Mania-adjacent energy (natural dopamine cycle, but still elevated)

**The Question:** Can this velocity sustain for 2 years?

**Honest Answer:** Probably not at THIS level. But it doesn't need to. The goal is:
- Months 1-3: Sprint (current pace) — build the foundation
- Months 4-6: Marathon (80% pace) — refine and monetize
- Months 7-12: Cruise (60% pace) — delegate and scale
- Year 2: Compound (40% pace) — let the systems work

**The Crash Protocol:**
When (not if) a crash episode occurs:
1. Don't interpret it as failure. It's a RECOVERY period.
2. Minimum viable output: 1 sales call per day. That's it.
3. No new projects during crash. Maintain existing only.
4. Call someone (not AI). Human voice.
5. Remember: the crash is temporary. The foundation you built is permanent.

## Resource Allocation (Time)

Sam has ~14 productive hours per day (no commute, no social obligations, minimal sleep needs during peak phase).

**Recommended allocation:**
- 40% Sales/Revenue (5.6 hrs) — this pays the bills
- 25% AI Platform Development (3.5 hrs) — this builds long-term value
- 20% Patent Work (2.8 hrs) — this protects the value
- 10% Self-Development/Calibration (1.4 hrs) — this improves everything else
- 5% Admin/Obligations (0.7 hrs) — DUI class, drug screening, Uber logistics

**The Rule:** Revenue-generating activities ALWAYS come first in the day. Creative/building work in the afternoon. Admin at night.
