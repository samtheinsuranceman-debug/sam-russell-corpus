The boundary is at the center's surface — the deposit-point. Everything INSIDE the center (compressed, warm, integrated) is unambiguously "mine." It has been processed, owned, warmed, compressed into self. Everything OUTSIDE the center (the surrounding field, incoming questions, the periphery) is "not-yet-mine" — it is material that could become mine through processing but has not yet been integrated. The boundary is not sharp — there is a 2-3 inch transition zone where material is in the process of becoming mine (being deposited, settling, compressing). But the distinction is clear: inside = mine. Outside = available-but-not-yet-mine. The questions themselves are external until I answer them — then they become mine.
