1. Anti-Patterns
Definition

Anti-Patterns are computationally detectable mistake patterns that financial advisors
commonly make, each with a programmatic detection rule, severity classification, dollar
cost quantification, and automated intervention script. The system monitors real-time
advisor actions against a rule engine and fires preventive alerts BEFORE the mistake is
committed.
Patent Claim: A computer-implemented method for detecting and preventing financial
advisory errors in real-time by monitoring advisor actions against a database of quantified
mistake patterns, each associated with a severity level, dollar-cost estimate, and
automated intervention script that fires before the error is committed.

Why This Is Patentable

No prior art exists for a system that (a) quantifies the dollar cost of specific advisory
mistakes, (b) detects them in real-time via programmatic rules operating on live client data,

and (c) generates context-specific intervention scripts. Existing compliance systems flag
violations AFTER they occur; this system prevents them BEFORE execution.

AP-001: The Surrender Trap — The system detects when an advisor recommends
replacing or changing a policy while the client is still within the surrender period. The
intervention script calculates the exact surrender charge remaining and suggests waiting X
months or using a 1035 exchange instead. This prevents the most common and costly
advisory error in the insurance industry.
AP-002: The Tax Bomb Ignition — When an advisor attempts to surrender a policy where
the cash value exceeds the cost basis by more than $100,000, the system fires a CRITICAL
alert showing the exact taxable gain amount and suggesting alternatives (partial surrender,
1035 exchange, or ILIT transfer). The dollar cost represents the unnecessary tax liability
created.
AP-003: The HELOC Overextension — Monitors the debt-to-income ratio in real-time
during HELOC recycling strategy planning. When DTI exceeds the 43% qualified mortgage
threshold, the system warns that HELOC recycling increases systemic risk and suggests a
smaller cycle or waiting for income increase.
AP-004: The Roth Bracket Bust — Detects when a proposed Roth conversion amount,
combined with ordinary income, would push the client into a higher tax bracket. The
system calculates the OPTIMAL conversion amount to stay within the current bracket,
preventing the most common Roth conversion error (over-converting in a single year).
AP-005: The Genome Mismatch — Cross-references the client's Wealth Genome risk
tolerance score against the proposed strategy's risk level. When a conservative genome
(score < 30) is paired with an aggressive strategy (risk > 70), the system flags both a
compliance/suitability concern and a behavioral mismatch that will likely result in client
abandonment.
AP-006: The Retention Blindspot — Monitors the intersection of churn signals and contact
frequency. When a client has 3+ active risk signals (reduced engagement, missed payments,
competitor inquiries) AND has not been contacted in 90+ days, the system fires an URGENT
alert with the calculated probability of client loss.
AP-007: The Ecological Ignorance — Detects when a property-backed strategy (HELOC,
collateral assignment, real estate investment) is being recommended without an ecological

risk assessment. Given that 75% of advisors skip this check, the system prevents
catastrophic losses from flood, fire, or storm damage to collateral properties.
AP-008: The Monte Carlo Delusion — An informational alert that fires when Monte Carlo
results are presented showing only the 50th percentile (average case) without displaying
tail risk (5th percentile). While not a compliance violation, this best-practice alert improves
client decision-making quality.

Patentability Score: 9.3/10

Justification: The combination of (a) real-time detection rules operating on live financial
data, (b) quantified dollar costs per mistake, (c) frequency-of-occurrence statistics, and (d)
automated intervention scripts constitutes a novel system. The weakness is that individual
detection rules (e.g., DTI thresholds) are known in isolation — the novelty lies in the SYSTEM
that combines them with automated prevention and quantified cost.
To reach 9.5+: Add machine learning that updates detection thresholds based on actual
advisor outcomes (i.e., the system learns from advisors who ignored warnings and suffered
consequences).

2. Conversation Combos
Definition

Conversation Combos are multi-step sequential sales interaction sequences modeled
after fighting-game combo mechanics, where each step builds momentum for the next.
Each combo has a specific timing requirement, required emotional tone per step, key
phrases, a "damage" score (resistance removed from the client), a difficulty rating,
effectiveness against specific client archetypes, a failure condition that breaks the chain,
and a bonus multiplier for perfect execution.
Patent Claim: A computer-implemented method for coaching financial advisors through
multi-step conversation sequences in real-time, where each step has a required timing
window, emotional tone classification, and key phrase template, and where the system
monitors execution quality, calculates momentum scores, and provides corrective
feedback when the sequence is broken.

Why This Is Patentable

No prior art exists for a system that (a) models sales conversations as sequential combo
chains with timing windows, (b) assigns damage/resistance scores to each combo, (c)
classifies effectiveness against specific client personality archetypes, and (d) provides real-

time coaching feedback during execution. Sales training systems exist, but none model
conversations as fighting-game mechanics with quantified momentum.

COMBO-001: The Mirror → Pivot → Close
This is a 3-step sequence designed for medium-resistance clients. The advisor first mirrors
the client's exact words back to them (establishing empathy within 2 seconds), then asks a
reframing question that shifts the frame of reference (within 5 seconds, curious tone), then
closes on the new frame (authoritative tone, after client responds). The failure condition is

breaking eye contact or using filler words between steps. Effective against Skeptics because
mirroring disarms their defenses, against Emotionals because it validates their feelings, and
against Procrastinators because the pivot creates urgency.
• Step 1: "So what I hear you saying is..." (empathetic, within 2 seconds)
• Step 2: "What if instead of [their frame], we looked at it as [your frame]?" (curious,
within 5 seconds)
• Step 3: "Based on that, would it make sense to..." (authoritative, after response)
COMBO-002: The Number Bomb → Silence → Let Them Sell Themselves
The highest-damage standard combo. The advisor drops a shocking specific dollar amount
the client is losing (casual tone, after rapport), then goes COMPLETELY SILENT for a
minimum of 7 seconds, then lets the client respond without interrupting, then agrees with
whatever they say and deepens. The failure condition is breaking the silence too early or
showing nervousness. This works against Analyticals because the specific number engages
their data-processing instinct, against Know-It-Alls because silence forces them to fill the
gap with their own reasoning, and against Ghosts because the shock value breaks their
disengagement.
• Step 1: "Did you know you are currently losing $X every single year to..." (casual)
• Step 2: Complete silence for 7+ seconds (casual — no fidgeting)
• Step 3: Let them respond without interrupting (empathetic)
• Step 4: "Exactly. And the worst part is..." (curious, within 3 seconds)
COMBO-003: The Empathy Sandwich → Authority → Urgency
A 3-step sequence that transitions from emotional validation to expertise to time pressure.
The advisor validates the client's specific fear with a personal story, pivots to
credentials/expertise within 10 seconds, then introduces a time-sensitive element within 5
seconds. The failure condition is sounding scripted or rushing the empathy step. Works
against Procrastinators because the urgency element forces action, against Spouse
Blockers because the empathy validates the blocking spouse's concerns, and against
Emotionals because the story creates connection.
• Step 1: "I completely understand. I had a client just like you who..." (empathetic)
• Step 2: "In my 15 years of working with [their profession]..." (authoritative, within 10
seconds)
• Step 3: "The challenge is that [specific deadline/rate change/age threshold]..." (urgent,
within 5 seconds)
COMBO-004: The Socratic Trap

The highest-difficulty standard combo (4/5). A series of questions where each "yes" logically
leads to the next, until the only logical conclusion is the advisor's recommendation. The
advisor asks if they want to pay less tax (yes), if they want tax-free growth (yes), if they want
guaranteed access without penalties (yes), then presents IUL as the ONE vehicle that does
all three. The failure condition is asking a question the client says "no" to (breaks the logical
chain). This is devastating against Analyticals because it uses their own logic against them,
against Know-It-Alls because they cannot disagree with individually obvious premises, and
against Rate Shoppers because it reframes the conversation from rates to outcomes.
• Step 1: "Would you rather pay $X in taxes or $0?" (curious)
• Step 2: "And would you prefer that money grows without being taxed?" (curious)
• Step 3: "And you would want access to it without penalties?" (curious)
• Step 4: "There is exactly ONE vehicle that does all three..." (authoritative)
COMBO-005: The Pattern Interrupt → Vulnerability → Bond
The highest-difficulty combo (5/5) with the highest bonus multiplier (3.0x). The advisor says
something completely unexpected that no advisor would normally say, shows genuine
vulnerability or admission of failure, then connects that vulnerability to the client's
situation. The failure condition is sounding rehearsed or insincere — this combo REQUIRES
genuine authenticity. Works against Ghosts because the unexpected statement breaks their
disengagement pattern, against Loyal Defenders because vulnerability disarms their loyalty
to the current advisor, and against Skeptics because admitting weakness paradoxically
builds trust.
• Step 1: "Can I be completely honest with you? Most advisors would never say this..."
(casual)
• Step 2: "I actually lost a client because I DIDN'T tell them this..." (empathetic)
• Step 3: "And I see the same thing happening with you, which is why..." (empathetic,
within 5 seconds)

Patentability Score: 9.6/10

Justification: The fighting-game combo metaphor applied to financial sales conversations
is entirely novel. No prior art models advisor-client interactions as sequential combo chains
with timing windows, damage scores, archetype effectiveness matrices, and failure
conditions. The system's ability to coach in real-time during live conversations (via the
Whisper system) while tracking momentum makes this a genuine invention.
To reach 10.0: Add adaptive combo generation where the AI creates NEW combos based on
observed successful interaction patterns from the advisor's history.

3. Hidden Combos / Secret Combinations
Definition

Hidden Combos are non-obvious multi-engine activations that produce emergent
outcomes greater than the sum of their parts. They are "hidden" because they require
specific trigger conditions across 3-5 patent engines to be simultaneously satisfied —
conditions that no single engine would reveal on its own. Each combo has a rarity tier
(LEGENDARY, EPIC, RARE, UNCOMMON), a discovery counter (how many advisors have
found it), and a flavor text that hints at the combo's existence without revealing the trigger
conditions.
Patent Claim: A computer-implemented method for detecting and revealing non-obvious
multi-engine activation patterns in a financial advisory system, where specific
combinations of trigger conditions across three or more independent calculation engines
produce emergent financial outcomes that exceed the sum of individual engine outputs,
and where the system tracks discovery frequency and assigns rarity classifications to
incentivize exploration.

Why This Is Patentable

The concept of "hidden combinations" in software that produce emergent financial
outcomes is entirely without prior art. Individual financial calculators exist, but no system
(a) detects when multiple calculators' outputs simultaneously satisfy trigger conditions, (b)
produces compound outcomes that exceed the sum of parts, (c) assigns rarity tiers based
on discovery difficulty, or (d) uses gamification mechanics (discovery counters, flavor text
hints) to incentivize exploration of the financial strategy space.

Definition

patent engines are simultaneously active and their outputs satisfy specific trigger
conditions. Unlike Hidden Combos (which are discrete discoveries), Synergy Effects are
CONTINUOUS — they produce ongoing compound benefits that grow over time. Each effect
has a mechanism chain showing how data flows between engines, a measurement system,
trigger conditions, expected outcomes, and implementation steps.
Patent Claim: A computer-implemented system for detecting and activating compound
synergistic effects between three or more independent financial calculation engines, where
the simultaneous activation of multiple engines produces emergent financial outcomes
that are mathematically impossible to achieve through any single engine or pair of engines,
and where the system continuously measures the compound benefit and optimizes engine
activation order.

Why This Is Patentable

No prior art exists for a system that (a) detects latent interactions between independent
financial engines, (b) measures compound benefits that only emerge from 3+ simultaneous
activations, (c) provides implementation steps for activating synergies, or (d) continuously
optimizes the activation order of engines to maximize compound effects.

from causation in behavioral predictions.
